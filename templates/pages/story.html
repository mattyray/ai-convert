My Journey

From the open sea to spinal cord injury — and from survival to service through art, code, and storytelling.

Who I Am

I’m Matthew Raynor — once a commercial fisherman, now a quadriplegic full-stack developer, artist, and writer. Six years ago, I broke my neck diving into shallow water. I lost all function from the chest down, including use of my hands. The physical pain was immense — but so was the emotional isolation that followed.

Still, I refused to give up. I rebuilt my life using styluses and voice commands to operate a computer, and taught myself how to code.

What I Do

I design and build full-stack Django web applications, often for small businesses. One of my major projects — EJ Art Mover — is a business management system for a professional art transport company. It handles logistics, invoices, scheduling, and client data in an elegant, mobile-friendly interface.

I also create and sell aluminum-mounted landscape photography and publish a visual memoir titled Before Me After Me, capturing my transformation through image alone. My work has been shown in galleries and featured in press outlets like ABC 7, Newsday, The Southampton Press, and more.

How I Inspire

I speak honestly about pain, healing, and purpose. My TikTok and Instagram videos blend inspiration and humor for others facing hardship — especially those with disabilities or health conditions. I also run a Substack newsletter, where I write about recovery, mindset, spiritual practice, and self-compassion.

I even started an AA meeting in my nursing home — because service doesn’t require mobility. It requires heart.
